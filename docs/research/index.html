Research

Research interests, affiliations, and ongoing projects

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of political science and computational social science. I am broadly interested in legislative behavior — how legislatures organize their work, how representatives vote and communicate, and what shapes the policy agenda. A central thread in this work is the use of text-as-data methods to analyze legislative corpora at scale: floor speeches, bills, committee reports, and written questions that together trace the preferences and strategies of political actors.

A second line of research concerns public opinion and political behavior. This includes survey methods, vote choice models, and the study of how citizens form and revise preferences about public policy. I am particularly interested in methodological questions about measurement and causal identification in observational political science.

Methodologically, I draw on machine learning, deep learning, causal inference, and survey methods. Much of my work involves building reproducible pipelines that integrate these tools with substantive social science questions — bridging the gap between methodological innovation and empirical political research.

Affiliations

Data Scientist @ AtlasIntel

AtlasIntel provides advanced data products for political intelligence and economic forecasting. My work involves calibrating survey samples, political analysis, public opinion monitoring, and improving internal data pipelines.

Researcher @ IESP-UERJ

MAPE · coordinated by Fernando Meireles & Bruno Schaefer

Policy and Election Monitoring and Evaluation Laboratory. Dedicated to studying public policy, elections, and their interconnections using causal inference and machine learning techniques.

DOXA · coordinated by Fernando Meireles & Argelina Cheibub Figueiredo

Laboratory of Electoral Studies, Political Communication and Public Opinion. A leading center for research on electoral processes, political communication, and voting behavior in Brazil.

NECON · coordinated by Fabiano Santos

Center for Studies on Congress. Focused on academic research on the Brazilian legislature, including databases on nominal votes, party migration, and legislative production.

Before joining AtlasIntel, MAPE, DOXA, and NECON, I provided data science and research consulting services to both industry (Roberto Marinho Foundation, Artplan, Visagio) and academia (FGV EBAPE, CEBRAP, GENI/UFF). See my CV for a full record of my professional experience.

A Systematic Review of Text-as-Data Approaches to Parliamentary Behavior

A structured, reproducible review of how computational text methods have been used to study legislative organization. The paper applies text-as-data tools to the review process itself, using abstract embeddings to map the field semantically. To be presented at XIII ALACIP (Buenos Aires, 2026).
