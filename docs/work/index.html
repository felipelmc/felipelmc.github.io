My Work

What I have been doing lately

Data Scientist @ AtlasIntel

AtlasIntel provides advanced data products for political intelligence and economic forecasting. I'm currently working on calibrating survey samples, political analysis, public opinion monitoring, and improving internal data pipelines.

Researcher @ MAPE (IESP-UERJ)

MAPE is the Policy and Election Monitoring and Evaluation Laboratory, coordinated by Fernando Meireles (IESP-UERJ) and Bruno Schaefer (IESP-UERJ). We are dedicated to studying public policy, elections, and their interconnections. Our goal is to improve academic production on the topic and decisively influence public debate. Specifically, we combine different methodologies to study policy and elections, including causal inference and machine learning techniques.

Researcher @ DOXA (IESP-UERJ)

DOXA is the Laboratory of Electoral Studies, Political Communication and Public Opinion, coordinated by Argelina Cheibub Figueiredo (IESP-UERJ). The laboratory is a leading center for research in political communication in Brazil. It is dedicated to the study of electoral processes and political communication, producing theses, dissertations, publications, and analyses across topics such as electoral advertising, political journalism, political attitudes, and voting behavior. More recently, it has expanded its scope to include the analysis of electoral results, broadening its research agenda and incorporating scholars dedicated to the study of elections.

Before joining AtlasIntel, MAPE, and DOXA, I provided data science and research consulting services to both industry (Roberto Marinho Foundation, Artplan, Visagio) and academia (FGV EBAPE, CEBRAP, GENI/UFF). Please check my CV for more information on my work experience.
